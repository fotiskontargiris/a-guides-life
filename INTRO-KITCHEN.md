# The Kitchen — Game Intro & Character Creation

*The opening scene. Doubles as character creation: the player sets a name, picks the
favourite dish (small bonus + recurring callback), and chooses which fallen hero they
are (the catastrophe reveal = the `BACKGROUNDS` choice). Voice: second person, present
tense, terse, warm, comic. No emojis. The `▸` lines are the "instruments" notes — the
mechanical hook surfaced diegetically; show or hide them, your call (see notes at end).*

---

## Beat 0 — Cold open

The bus from the airport smelled of diesel and someone's gardenia. Now you are at the
kitchen table in Kalamata, in the chair you sat in at seven, at twelve, at seventeen
swearing you would never come back.

The oilcloth has the same lemons on it.

Something is browning in the oven. The good plates are out — the ones for guests — which
is how you know she has decided you are a guest now. Or, worse, a patient.

"Sit, sit," your mother says, though you are already sitting. "You've gotten thin. What
did they do to you out there."

It is not a question. Nothing she says tonight will be a question.

> **▸ What does she call you?**  *[name entry]*
>
> She tries the name out loud, the way she has every day of your life, and adds the
> diminutive she has never once dropped. *Paidí mou.* My child.

## Beat 1 — The food *(pick your favourite dish)*

She sets it down in front of you. Your favourite — and she will not hear that you have a
different favourite now, that people change. Tonight you are the child who loved this, so
this is what love looks like, steaming, with too much of it.

Choose one. *(She will make it again, all game, "since you're clearly not eating properly
out there.")*

- **Gemistá** — peppers and tomatoes stuffed with rice, the ones you used to pick the
  filling out of. *▸ You start a little more rested.*
- **Pastítsio** — three slabs, because two would be an insult. *▸ A small cushion of
  goodwill; the world is gentler with you at first.*
- **Bakaliáros with skordaliá** — fried cod and a garlic dip strong enough to clear a
  trailhead. *▸ You shrug off the rough days a touch better.*
- **Youvétsi** — lamb and orzo, the Sunday dish on a Tuesday, which means she is genuinely
  worried. *▸ A few extra euros pressed into your hand "for the bus."*

## Beat 2 — The catastrophe *(pick your hero)*

She lets you eat three bites. Then the wooden spoon comes up, and waves at the whole shape
of your life.

"And the… the thing. Out there. It's finished?"

You tell her what happened.

**① The almost-doctor.** "Three years of medical school," she tells the icon over the
door, not you. "*Three years.*" You don't correct her to four failed anatomy exams. You
tell her the truth instead: you were always good with people in pain — just never with the
test that was supposed to prove it. *▸ Injuries and frightened clients are safe in your
hands; but you'll scrub a risky day a braver guide would run.*

**② The Mykonos chef.** "The restaurant was in a *magazine*," she informs the spoon. You
leave out the rent, the waiter, the stolen password. You came home with a tattoo of a sea
bream and the only gift that survived: making strangers feel fed and looked after. *▸ Feed
the group and the tips and the reviews follow — but mind the provisions, or they eat your
margin.*

**③ The army washout.** She still keeps the selection photo on the dresser, the one where
you are all mud and certainty. You don't mention the final day. You tell her you can read a
sky like a sentence. *▸ You run the storms and the high routes others cancel — but you
frighten the gentle ones.*

**④ The deckhand.** "My captain," she says — which you never quite were. Years of salt and
other men's cargo, and a stomach that has had enough of the open sea. But water still reads
to you like family. *▸ The coast and the rivers pay well in your hands; the dry heights
don't.*

**⑤ The influencer.** She never understood what you did, only that it had numbers, and the
numbers went away. Still — you can make any ordinary afternoon look like the best day of a
stranger's life. *▸ Reputation climbs fast and reviews multiply — but a bad day travels
just as far.*

**⑥ The startup engineer.** "A company. In *Germany.*" She says Germany the way other
people say the moon. You built something elegant for eleven users; now you will build it
for goats. *▸ Your gear lasts and your projects pay — but you'll need time before strangers
warm to you.*

**⑦ The philologist.** Four languages, and a man named Babis who got the post. You can hand
a German a poem and give a Frenchman back his own childhood. *▸ Foreign groups adore you and
tip the difference — if you can stop lecturing.*

**⑧ The Fasaia.** She has braced herself for the words "energy" and "alignment," and she is
bearing them like weather. Goa rearranged you. The bank account did not approve. *▸ You stay
calm when the day doesn't — but your peace is slow to push for the sale.*

**⑨ The Alani.** She tells the neighbours you have "many friends." This is true. Some of
them you owe. You know every back door in the prefecture and the price of everything in it.
*▸ Cheap gear, easy deals — but the merchandise is sometimes of uncertain provenance.*

**⑩ The Greek Villager.** This one she cannot spin, because the whole village watched it
go — the flock, the olive grove, the *kafeneío* where the beach bar stands now. The most
local man alive, reduced to selling his own backyard to foreigners. *▸ Every path, every
cousin, every village deal is yours and cheap — but the foreigners and their languages are
a wall you will have to learn to climb.*

---

## Beat 3 — The pitch

She lets you finish. She crosses herself, once, toward the icon. And then — as if the idea
has only just floated down to her, as if she has not been building toward it since the
arrivals hall — she says it.

"You know what you should do."

You know exactly what she is about to say.

"*Tourism.* Look around you — is there anywhere more beautiful in this world? The foreigners
come with their money and their little walking sticks and they *pay* — they pay, paidí mou —
to see what we see for free every day of our lives. And you have the languages. Take them up
the mountain. Show them Polylímnio. Show them the sea."

She is already clearing your plate, though you are not finished.

"Kosta's boy does it, up in Stoúpa. He bought a car." A pause, the knife going in clean.
"Tomorrow you begin. I've already told Kyría Voúla you're in tourism now."

It is decided. It was always decided.

---

## Beat 4 — Step out *(into the panigiri, → game begins)*

Outside, the village is tuning up. It is August; down the lane a clarinet finds its note,
and the smell of grilling meat is quietly winning its war with the night jasmine. The
*panigíri*.

You have a name, a mother who has already told the neighbours, and — somewhere out past the
lit-up square — a whole country of mountains you spent your life ignoring.

*Per-hero send-off as you step into the festival (one line each):*

- **almost-doctor** — Kyría Voúla finds you in ninety seconds, blood-pressure diary in hand.
- **chef** — you taste the spit-roast from across the square and silently rebalance the salt.
- **army washout** — you clock three exits and the one unattended gas bottle. Old habits.
- **deckhand** — someone mentions the sea and you are nine sentences into a story off Yemen.
- **influencer** — you film the clarinet player. He has more presence than your last sponsor.
- **engineer** — you find yourself estimating the throughput of the souvláki line.
- **philologist** — you correct a tourist's Greek, gently, and they hug you.
- **Fasaia** — your uncle presses the first tsípouro into your hand. *Breathe,* you think. It
  is 9pm. By 2am your inner peace will be in open combat with a brass section.
- **Alani** — three people wave. Two of them you owe. You wave back at all three.
- **Villager** — you know the clarinet player, his father, and the debt his father owed yours.

## Implementation notes

- This replaces the current title → profile flow. The three player inputs map straight to
  `initGame(name, background, startingItems)`:
  - **name** → Beat 0.
  - **hero** → the `background` id (the ten extend/replace today's four `BACKGROUNDS`:
    `medic`→①, plus new ids for ②–⑩). Each carries the mechanical hook in its `perk`/`spec`.
  - **favourite dish** → a tiny modifier (extra `energy`, a `rep` cushion, a `gear`/recovery
    nudge, or starting `cash`), stored on `S` as `S.dish` so the recurring callback can fire.
- The `▸` instruments lines can render in the Spline Sans Mono "instruments" style, or be
  hidden behind a "what does this mean?" reveal so first-time players meet their hero as a
  *story* and only then as a *stat*. Recommend showing them — the builds are part of the joke.
- Open question: **gendered diminutive.** Greek splits *yio mou* (son) / *kóri mou*
  (daughter); I used the neutral *paidí mou* throughout so no gender toggle is required. If
  you add one, the diminutives (and a few of mum's lines) can flex off it.
- Bump `SAVE_KEY` when `S.dish` and the expanded `background` ids land; add defensive
  defaults in `doContinue()` per CLAUDE.md §6.
